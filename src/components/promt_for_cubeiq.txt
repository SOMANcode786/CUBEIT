3. THE MOST IMPORTANT MOTION PRINCIPLE
SCROLL MUST CONTROL STATE — NOT JUST TRIGGER ANIMATIONS

Most important animations should be tied to scroll progress.

Scrolling down:

→ progresses the visual system forward.

Scrolling upward:

→ naturally reverses the same system.

Example:

If an SVG line draws from A → B → C while scrolling down,

then scrolling upward must:

C → B → A

and progressively erase/retract the line.

Do not make scroll animations that simply fire once and remain completed.

For narrative sections, build a single coordinated GSAP timeline controlled by ScrollTrigger progress.

Prefer:

scrubbed timelines
reversible progress
pinned storytelling where justified
continuous interpolation

over:

dozens of unrelated onEnter animations
independent competing ScrollTriggers
one-shot animations everywhere

Every major scene should feel physically attached to scroll.
